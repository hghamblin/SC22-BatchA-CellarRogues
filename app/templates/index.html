OVERVIEW

Input a prompt to generate an entertaining caption for social media!

Click here to try our generator!


Data Sets

During the creation of this generator our team used various data sets to create these unique captions. The tow main ones used are a poems and other users Instagram captions. We wanted to make sure our captions are as human as possible so people couldn't tell the difference if it was AI generated. Real Instagram captions from other users help achieve that by being able to express all kinds of emotion. The poem data sets helps to by being able to express the poetic side some captions include.

Learning Curves

Over the three weeks of developing this generator many problems occurred. Both profanity filters and appropriate emojis were a challenge. We didin't want our generator to include inappropriate language since we want our product to be age appropriate and be used by anyone. Same applies to the emojis we didn't want a caption to have a emoji that didn't fit well with the mood. If the caption is intended to be sad it wouldn't make sense for their to be a happy face. With quick thinking and hark work both of these problems were resolved.

Unique Features

The team wanted to have fun and unique features to ensure the best experience users can have. Using our data sets we divided them into two separate groups. The Instagram captions, and poems are used in the first one, while the lyrics with the previous two are added to the funny one. We also wanted to include both appropriate captions and emojis based off the users caption, that can be toggled on and off.

THE PROCESS

The Cellar Rogue's journey to building their very own, working AI Natural Language Processing model.

  • June 6 - June 10

    Week 1

    The first week of AI camp was used as an introduction to the coding language, python. Students attended lectures in the morning and afternoon about aspects of coding and python, such as preprocessing, machine learning, neural networks, etc. Students practiced coding by working on mini projects in breakout rooms with their groups. The Cellar Rogues also brainstormed ideas of models they wanted to create and decided upon a caption generator that would be based off of corresponding data sets they found. On June 10, the Cellar Rogues presented their product spec/model outline and received positive responses!

  • June 13 - June 17

    Week 2

    During the second week of AI camp, the Cellar Rogues began to develop their model. They continued to attend lectures everyday while usually spending the afternoon on writing the code for the model. They discussed what they wanted —and didn't want— their model to include. When preprocessing their data, the Cellar Rogues decided to exclude profanity, names, @'s, etc. By Wednesday, the Cellar Rogues had a working model! They spent the rest of the week fine tuning the model.

  • June 20 - June 24

    Week 3

    During the third week of AI Camp, the Cellar Rogues started developing the website for their model. The Cellar Rogues collaborated to choose a bootstrap theme and aesthetic for the website and discussed what they should include on the website. The Cellar Rogues spent June 21-22 working on the website and adding finishing touches to the model. On June 23, the Cellar Rogues prepared a presentation slideshow for their model and created a demo video. On June 24, the Cellar Rogues finished their model and website and presented it to AI camp!
